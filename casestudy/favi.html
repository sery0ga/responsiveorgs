General

FAVI is based in Hallencourt, northern France, in the French region of the “Picardie”. It was founded in 1957 and has today over 800 associates (not employees!) with a turnover of about 91 million. € (30% from export). Its product areas include the design, optimization, validation, die-casting, machining, assembly and marketing of all components that can be made of die-cast copper alloys. In this special market niche it is the world leader. The main customers are from the automotive industry e.g. PSA (Citroen, Peugeot), Renault, Fiat, VW, Audi, Volvo. The main products with an European market share of over 50% are automotive gear boxes.
from case study by International Center for Outperformance

FAVI has been awarded in 1997 the fist European foundry to be certified ISO 14001, in 2000, the first French company to be certified OHSAS 18001, in 2002 the first French company to be certified Q.S.E. and in 2008 the first European foundry to be certified ILO-OSH 2001.

FAVI has an astonishing record of 0 delivery delays for 30 years (since 1984). "FAVI has reduced the cost of producing a gearbox fork by 40% in real terms over two decades".

Roles / Structure

FAVI is divided to 21 _mini-factories_. There are 5 service factories:

  • Commercial
  • Studies
  • Laboratory
  • Quality
  • Administration

and 16 production ones: - General maintenance, building of machines - Production of moulds and tools - Maintenance of moulds - Foundry of automotive parts - Foundry of other types of parts - PSA Valenciennes - PSA Borny - Citroën - Volkswagen - Audi Getrag - Renault - Fiat - Volvo - Diverse electric parts, locks, sanitary... - Water meters - Copper rotors

Each production factory is in direct relation with:

  • a client (Fiat, Citroën, Volkswagen...)
  • a sector of activity (water meters, rotors...)

"[If] a client would develop in a new activity large enough we would create a specific mini factory for that client", writes Jean-Francois Zobrist

Mini-factory

Each mini-factory is composed of 20-35 operators. It is physically co-located, has own identity (floor and machine colour, logo) and has an elected leader.

Mini-factory is autonomous and responsible for planning, production and sale of its services or products.

Orders arrive directly in the shop floor, are executed and only after go back to administrative department for treatment
from "The Story Of FAVI" by Jean-Francois Zobrist

Team Leader

The leaders are elected by mini-factory members and are always former operators. The leaders are equal to operators and stay with them. Their responsibilities include but not limited to: security and health at work, salaries and their increase, definition of hiring needs, welcome and follow-up of newcomers, training and investment plans.

Though it may seem like a traditional manager but the difference is huge:

  • the leaders don't tell operators what to do and how to do their job
  • the leaders take an administrative burden
  • the leaders make all important decision with their teams
  • operators could vote with their feet and leave a leader they don't like

At the same time the leaders don't need to report to anyone except their operators and clients. They choose themselves which indicators they wish to follow.

His goal: maintain the existing jobs thanks to a permanent progress systemic implementation in the social, societal, economic and environmental fields.
His sanction: loose business and staff due to his lack of creativeness or reactivity.

The Merchant / The Sales Representative

The merchant's role is to bring the outside in, by conveying the maximum information from the customer to his/hers operators, on the strategy, new products, changes, etc. And vice versa to pass information from the mini-plants back to the client. This is done usually formally during a monthly meeting of the mini-plant where the merchant provides all information of the new client, and vice versa by informal tours through the mini-plants. Sometimes the merchant calls the customer to come directly to the operators to explain a particular point. Whenever possible the merchant puts all associates of his/hers mini-plant in a car and goes with them to the customer.

The sales representatives have no rewards, no premiums, no objectives and no reports to do. There is only a pressure from the client. They may contact internal experts about particular issues or CEO about new business but it's up to them to make a final decision.

His goal: create jobs with human and financial added value
His sanction: have to fire some employees
from "The Story Of FAVI" by Jean-Francois Zobrist

Story of the temporary workers we kept

1990, first gulf war. Demand crisis, drop of quantity of orders

We had step by step reduced the production, used the opportunity of the lower activity to paint, clean, order, accelerate the training plans, but around February-March these measures were not sufficient any more.

We were at that time around 300 and employed as well around 30 temporary workers. Temporary work has always been for us the preliminary step to full employment, and a way to adapt to the numerous constraints of our laws. Usually, temporary workers had two contracts of three months, after what the best were offered a one year contract within the frame of the laws at that time: integration contract, qualifying contract...

This system allowed us to have an 18 months trial period before to sign an unlimited working contract. In France, this unlimited working contract is more binding than a wedding contract as it is easier to divorce than to end a working contract with an employee.

These eighteen months were structured with programmed integration steps according to our document "Integration of new workers".

So to manage the lower activity, not knowing what to do, I decided to gather the entire staff to search for a solution together. They had prepared for me a podium with empty pallets and I exposed to the tribe the current situation, what had already been done, the extremely high level of stock and I concluded explaining that we had around thirty temporary workers from whom we could resign very easily, but that it was a very sad decision, and a sort of discrimination for these young boys and girls who we wanted to integrate as new collaborators.

A game of questions and answers started among the different possible solutions and at a certain moment, Gerard, an operator in his fifties asked: "And if we stopped to work one week without being paid, could we keep temporary workers longer?"

I answered that yes, it could allow us to keep them longer, maybe one month, maybe more, we had to calculate. This would allow us to wait for the end of the crisis, that would of course come, as we all knew that a demand crisis based on collective fear never last long.

After this debate, I proposed to the entire staff to vote. At my stupefaction all hands went up!

I decided to stop production for a week, everyone would stay at home, except management and middle management who would work without salary during that week, being understood that when activity would come back, we would work back full Saturdays to recover the lost salaries.

This was done in the following months.

from "The Story Of FAVI" by Jean-Francois Zobrist

Salary Increases (by Jean-Francois Zobrist)

As within our system the leader decides for operators, the oldest sales manager for the youngest, well always someone working 8 hours per day directly in contact with his team, there was never any claim that came back to me, even for those getting 0%!

This notion of quiet friendly consensus is very important in terms of productivity because nothing costs more than rumours like:

"As usual, the boss is always supporting sales department!"

or

"It is always foundry that is allowed to invest!"

This kind of rumour costs 4 to 5 points of productivity all the time it is running.

Investments / Budgets

Each year in September I send a message to everyone reminding our company strategy and I propose that everyone, within his field and work position, think over the investments to do the following year in terms of comfort, safety, health at work, respect of the environment, quality, productivity and all this without defining a financial limit to foster creativity.

Each Mini-factory with the support of its productivity godfather and leader defines a workgroup to elaborate the investment budget. In November all this work is coming back to me. We sort between costs and investment and 8 times out of 10 the global amount is possible, so the plans of each mini-factory are accepted without any discussion!

We can also notice that 10 times out of 10 at the end of the budget year only 80% of forecasts have been achieved. And this only because Man being naturally good, what was in good faith forecasted a few months before seemed finally unnecessary according to the current financial year realization. 2 times out of 10 then the amount asked seems to me unreasonable. In this case I gather all the managers and tell them: "You asked me for 12 but I truly believe that we can not reasonably do more than 10."

Then a meeting between leaders is organized and by "bargaining" a natural consensus is reached among all guided by their global knowledge of the investment plan that they did not have beforehand.

This allows in good faith and with good will to grant priority to what serves best the collective interest.

One day, an automotive client, "square", comes to make a logistics audit. During the opening meeting, two engineers, a man and a woman, senior, tell me that they will stay for three days. However, after half a day they ask to meet me. They come with very disturbed faces and signs of anger: - "It is not going well at all! We have never seen this! There are no indicators to follow delays, no indicators to follow shortages, you have no structure, no planning department, no launching department, no scheduling, no management of delays, no service level follow-up for your clients... "So why do you expect me to implement indicators following something that does not exist?" Answer: "..." In the meantime I had drawn on papers all the indicators they wanted, all showing a beautiful line at zero and I gave them the sheets saying: - "Now, if you wish, every month we will send you updated tables like those, and I really expect they will remain identical to these ones!"
